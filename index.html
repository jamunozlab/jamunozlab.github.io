jamunozlab

  computational physics
  at The University of Texas at El Paso

  Our group develops scientific theories and computational methods
  to understand the many facets of thermodynamics, including chaos
  and systems out of equilibrium, both in the atomistic and continuum
  regimes.

  Some of the pipelines that we develop are machine learning or
  mathematical optimization techniques that we combine with code
  that simulates physical phenomena, while others make a connection
  between the parameter space of learning models and the phase space
  of physics.

  We leverage mentoring, camaraderie, and the pursuit of science for
  the personal and professional growth of our members and contribute
  to the educational and economic development of the El Paso del Norte
  region.

 This website is work in progress.
